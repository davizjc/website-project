Khao Moo Daeng (Red Pork w/ Red Sauce)

6 serves

Ingredient

Pork

  • 2 pork tenderloins (about 2 lbs total, cut in half).
  • 6 cups cooked jasmine rice (2 cups uncooked)

    • Pork Marinade

  • 2 Tbsp Chinese cooking wine / rice wine
  • 2 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 Tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp light soy sauce
  • 3 Tbsp tomato sauce (or ketchup)

    • Rice Sauce

  • 1 Tbsp tapioca starch
  • 1 cup of water

    • Garnish

  • 2 eggs (boiled, peeled and cut in half lengthwise)
  • Coriander leaves
  • Cucumber and scallions sliced

    • Procedure

    1. In a Ziploc bag, combine the Chinese cooking wine, sesame oil, sugar, salt, soy sauce and tomato sauce. Add pork tenderloin and marinate in a fridge for at least two hours and up to 6 hours for best results.

    2. Heat a wok or a cast iron pan over medium heat. Add the pork and the marinade, and cook and bring to gentle boil. Cover and simmer , occasionally stirring, for about 5-7 minute. Flip the meat and continue simmering for another 5-7 minutes.

    Rice Sauce

    1. Dissolve tapioca starch in 1 cup of water and add to the pan with the marinade leftovers after meat simmering. Bring to boil and simmer until the sauce thickens.

    Serve

    1. To serve, slice the pork about 1/4 inch thick, place on top of rice bed and dress with the sauce. Garnish with slices of cucumber, scallions and fresh coriander leaves.
