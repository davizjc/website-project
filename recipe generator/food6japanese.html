Spicy Miso Tsukemen (Dipping Noodles)

2 serves

Ingredient

  • 6 ounces green tea
  • Ramen udon or somen noodles
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • 1 small shallot (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger (peeled and finely chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste
  • 1 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon powdered chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

    • Procedure

      1.In a pot over medium heat, add canola oil, garlic, ginger and shallot and cook for 3-4 minutes, until shallots are translucent.
      2.Add miso paste and soy sauce and stir until miso turns into a soft paste.
      3.Add water, sugar and chicken stock, stir and bring to a boil.
      4.Lower to a strong simmer and leave uncovered for 15 minutes.
      5.Turn the heat off and add chili oil, sesame oil and sesame seeds.
      6.Transfer the sauce to a tupperware container and refrigerate for at least an hour.
      7.Cook noodles according to direction on the package. Drain, rinse under cold water and drain well.
      8.Divide noodles among two plates or bowls.
      9.Divide tsukemen sauce among two other bowls and top with scallions, sesame seeds and bonito flakes. Serve.
